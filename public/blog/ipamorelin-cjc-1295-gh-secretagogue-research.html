Ipamorelin + CJC-1295 Research: Dual GH Secretagogue Mechanism

Published by Reptides.co Research Team · For in-vitro research reference only

Ipamorelin and CJC-1295 (no DAC) represent two distinct mechanisms of growth hormone axis stimulation that are frequently studied in combination. Their complementary receptor targets — ghrelin receptor (GHSR) and GHRH receptor respectively — make them a standard research pair in GH secretagogue investigations.

Ipamorelin: Ghrelin Receptor Agonist

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide (Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH₂) with a molecular weight of approximately 711.9 Da. It acts as a selective growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHSR/ghrelin receptor) agonist. In-vitro studies have noted its selectivity for GH release with minimal observed effects on cortisol, prolactin, or ACTH in pituitary cell culture models — a selectivity profile that distinguishes it from earlier GHSR agonists like GHRP-6 and GHRP-2.

CJC-1295 (no DAC): GHRH Analog

CJC-1295 without Drug Affinity Complex, also known as Mod-GRF(1-29), is a 29 amino acid analog of growth hormone releasing hormone. Four amino acid substitutions at positions 2, 8, 15, and 27 confer resistance to enzymatic degradation while maintaining GHRH receptor binding affinity. Molecular formula: C₁₅₂H₂₅₂N₄₄O₄₂, molecular weight approximately 3,367.9 Da.

Complementary Mechanism Rationale

The research rationale for studying these compounds together stems from their targeting of different receptor systems in the GH axis:

CJC-1295 activates the GHRH receptor on somatotroph cells, initiating GH gene transcription and secretion. Ipamorelin activates the ghrelin receptor, which amplifies GH release through a distinct intracellular signaling cascade (phospholipase C pathway vs. cAMP pathway). In cell culture models, simultaneous activation of both pathways has been studied for potential synergistic effects on GH secretion that exceed the sum of individual pathway stimulation.

Research Blend Format

CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin is available as a pre-blended lyophilized formulation (typically 10mg total peptide content) for researchers who want to study both pathways simultaneously without preparing separate solutions. This eliminates dilution calculation errors and ensures consistent ratios across experimental replicates.

Related GH Secretagogues

Researchers comparing secretagogue mechanisms may also investigate tesamorelin (44 amino acid GHRH analog with N-terminal modification), sermorelin (GHRH 1-29 without substitutions), and GHRP-2/GHRP-6 (hexapeptide GHSR agonists with broader hormonal effects than ipamorelin).
