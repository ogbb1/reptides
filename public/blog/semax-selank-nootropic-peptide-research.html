Semax and Selank: Russian Nootropic Peptide Research Overview

Published April 8, 2026 · 7 min read

Semax and Selank are synthetic heptapeptides developed at the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences. Both peptides are modifications of endogenous regulatory peptides engineered for enhanced stability and CNS bioavailability, and represent some of the most well-characterized nootropic peptides in research literature.

Semax: ACTH(4-10) Analog

Semax (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) is a synthetic analog of the ACTH(4-10) fragment with a C-terminal Pro-Gly-Pro stabilizing sequence. Unlike full-length ACTH, Semax lacks adrenocorticotropic activity — it does not stimulate cortisol release. Instead, its research interest focuses on neurotrophic factor modulation and cognitive signaling pathways.

Research demonstrates Semax increases expression of Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF) and its receptor TrkB in hippocampal and cortical neuron models. BDNF/TrkB signaling activates downstream cascades including PI3K/Akt, MAPK/ERK, and PLCγ pathways, all implicated in synaptic plasticity, long-term potentiation (LTP), and neurogenesis research.

Semax also modulates the serotonergic system through effects on tryptophan hydroxylase expression and 5-HT receptor signaling. Additional research examines its interaction with NGF (Nerve Growth Factor) expression and dopaminergic pathway modulation.

Selank: Tuftsin Analog

Selank (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro) is a synthetic analog of the endogenous immunomodulatory tetrapeptide tuftsin (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg) with the same Pro-Gly-Pro C-terminal stabilizing extension as Semax. Tuftsin is naturally derived from IgG heavy chain proteolysis by splenic enzymes.

Selank research spans both neuroactive and immunomodulatory domains. Anxiolytic-like effects in behavioral models are attributed to modulation of GABAergic neurotransmission, with studies showing enhanced GABA receptor sensitivity and altered chloride channel kinetics. Selank also influences the expression of interleukin genes (IL-6, IL-10) and enkephalin system components.

Gene expression studies reveal Selank modulates 45 genes in hippocampal tissue, with significant clusters in GABA receptor subunit expression, inflammation regulation, and neuropeptide processing pathways.

Dihexa and Other Cognitive Peptides

Dihexa (N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6)-aminohexanoic amide) is a hexapeptide derivative of angiotensin IV studied as an allosteric modulator of the hepatocyte growth factor (HGF)/c-Met receptor system. Research examines its effects on synaptic connectivity and dendritic spine density in hippocampal neuron models. Dihexa is available in 5mg and 10mg formulations.

DSIP (Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide, Trp-Ala-Gly-Gly-Asp-Ala-Ser-Gly-Glu) is a 9 amino acid neuropeptide studied in sleep architecture research, EEG pattern modulation, and stress-response signaling. Pinealon (Glu-Asp-Arg) is a tripeptide studied for pineal gland function and melatonin synthesis regulation.
