GHK-Cu and AHK-Cu: Copper Peptide Research in ECM Remodeling and Gene Expression

Published April 7, 2026 · 6 min read

GHK-Cu (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine:Copper(II)) is a naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide found in human plasma, saliva, and urine. First identified in 1973, GHK-Cu has since been recognized as a potent modulator of extracellular matrix remodeling and gene expression, with research demonstrating effects on over 4,000 human genes.

Copper Peptide Biochemistry

GHK has a high affinity for copper(II) ions (log K = 16.44 at pH 7.4), forming a stable 1:1 GHK:Cu²⁺ complex. The copper binding occurs through the glycyl amino terminus, the histidyl imidazole nitrogen, and the deprotonated amide nitrogen between glycine and histidine. This coordination geometry is critical for biological activity, as copper-free GHK shows significantly reduced effects in most research assays.

AHK-Cu (Alanine-Histidine-Lysine:Copper(II)) is a related copper-chelating tripeptide with alanine replacing glycine at the N-terminus. This substitution alters the copper coordination geometry and binding kinetics, providing a structural analog for comparative copper peptide research.

Extracellular Matrix Remodeling

GHK-Cu stimulates collagen synthesis (types I, III, and V) in fibroblast culture models while simultaneously promoting controlled matrix degradation through metalloproteinase (MMP-2, MMP-9) modulation. This balanced remodeling activity distinguishes GHK-Cu from simple collagen stimulators, enabling organized rather than fibrotic tissue architecture in research models.

Research demonstrates GHK-Cu increases decorin expression, a proteoglycan that regulates collagen fibrillogenesis. It also stimulates production of glycosaminoglycans including dermatan sulfate and heparan sulfate, key ECM structural components.

Gene Expression Modulation

Genome-wide expression profiling studies (Broad Institute Connectivity Map) reveal GHK-Cu modulates expression of approximately 32% of human genes. Key pathways affected include TGF-β superfamily signaling, Wnt pathway components, Notch signaling, insulin/IGF pathway genes, and DNA damage repair genes (particularly the ubiquitin-proteasome pathway).

Notable gene expression changes include upregulation of TGFB1, FGF2, VEGFA, and multiple collagen genes, alongside downregulation of pro-inflammatory NF-κB targets and metalloproteinase inhibitors (TIMPs). This broad transcriptomic signature makes GHK-Cu a subject of interest in systems biology and network pharmacology research.
