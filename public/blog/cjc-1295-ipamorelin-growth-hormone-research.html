CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin: The Gold Standard GH Secretagogue Stack for Research

Published April 2, 2026 · 8 min read

The combination of CJC-1295 (Modified GRF 1-29) and Ipamorelin represents one of the most extensively studied growth hormone secretagogue stacks in peptide research. This overview examines the individual mechanisms, synergistic effects, and comparative advantages of this combination versus other GH-axis peptides.

CJC-1295: Modified Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone Analog

CJC-1295 is a 29 amino acid synthetic analog of endogenous GHRH (Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone) with four amino acid substitutions at positions 2, 8, 15, and 27. These modifications confer resistance to enzymatic degradation by dipeptidyl peptidase-IV (DPP-IV), extending the biological half-life from approximately 7 minutes (native GHRH) to over 30 minutes for the no-DAC variant.

The Drug Affinity Complex (DAC) variant features a maleimidopropionic acid linker enabling covalent binding to serum albumin, extending the half-life to approximately 8 days. CJC-1295 without DAC (also called Modified GRF 1-29 or MOD-GRF) is preferred in research protocols requiring pulsatile GH release patterns that more closely mimic physiological secretion.

CJC-1295 acts at the GHRH receptor (GHRH-R) on anterior pituitary somatotrophs, activating adenylyl cyclase and increasing intracellular cAMP. This triggers protein kinase A (PKA) phosphorylation cascades that ultimately stimulate GH gene transcription and secretory granule exocytosis.

Ipamorelin: Selective Growth Hormone Secretagogue

Ipamorelin is a pentapeptide (Aib-His-D-2Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2) that acts as a selective agonist at the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R1a), also known as the ghrelin receptor. Unlike earlier GHS-R agonists such as GHRP-6 and GHRP-2, ipamorelin demonstrates high selectivity for GH release with minimal stimulation of cortisol, ACTH, or prolactin secretion in research models.

Ipamorelin's selectivity profile makes it particularly valuable in controlled research settings where confounding hormonal variables need to be minimized. Its short half-life of approximately 2 hours allows for precise temporal control of GH release pulses.

Synergistic Mechanisms

The CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin combination exploits complementary signaling pathways. CJC-1295 provides a sustained GHRH receptor stimulus (the "accelerator"), while Ipamorelin provides a GHS-R1a ghrelin-mimetic pulse (the "amplifier"). Simultaneously, Ipamorelin inhibits somatostatin release, removing the physiological "brake" on GH secretion.

Research demonstrates that combining a GHRH analog with a GHSR agonist produces GH output greater than the additive effects of either compound alone, suggesting true pharmacological synergy at the level of the anterior pituitary.

Comparison with Other GH Secretagogues

Sermorelin

Sermorelin is the original GHRH(1-29) analog without amino acid substitutions. While it maintains full GHRH receptor agonist activity, it is rapidly degraded by DPP-IV, resulting in a short half-life. Sermorelin is available in 2mg and 10mg formulations.

Tesamorelin

Tesamorelin is a 44 amino acid GHRH analog with a trans-3-hexenoic acid modification at the N-terminus. This modification enhances receptor binding affinity and provides moderate DPP-IV resistance. The Tesamorelin + Ipamorelin blend offers an alternative to the CJC-1295 combination.

GHRP-2 and GHRP-6

GHRP-2 (Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide-2) and GHRP-6 are hexapeptide GHS-R agonists with less selectivity than Ipamorelin. GHRP-6 notably increases ghrelin-like appetite signaling and cortisol release, while GHRP-2 has intermediate selectivity. Both are available in 5mg and 10mg research formulations.
