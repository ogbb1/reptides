HCG, Kisspeptin-10, and Gonadorelin: Reproductive Peptide Research Guide

Published April 9, 2026 · 7 min read

Reproductive signaling peptides targeting the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis represent a critical category of research compounds. This guide covers the primary peptides studied in LH/FSH modulation, GnRH receptor pharmacology, and upstream kisspeptin signaling research.

HCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin)

HCG is a heterodimeric glycoprotein hormone (36-40 kDa) composed of an alpha subunit shared with LH, FSH, and TSH, and a unique beta subunit conferring receptor specificity. HCG binds the LH/CG receptor (LHCGR), a G-protein coupled receptor expressed on gonadal cells, activating adenylyl cyclase and downstream cAMP/PKA signaling.

Research applications include LHCGR binding kinetics, steroidogenesis pathway activation studies, and comparative glycoprotein hormone structure-function analysis. Kisspeptin-10

Kisspeptin-10 is the C-terminal decapeptide fragment of full-length kisspeptin-54 (metastin). It binds the GPR54 (KISS1R) receptor on GnRH neurons in the hypothalamic arcuate and AVPV nuclei. Kisspeptin signaling is the primary upstream activator of pulsatile GnRH release, making it a master regulator of the HPG axis.

Kisspeptin-10 retains full receptor binding affinity despite being truncated from the 54 amino acid parent peptide. Research applications include GnRH pulse generator characterization, puberty onset signaling studies, and HPG axis feedback mechanism research. The kisspeptin/GnRH/LH cascade represents the fundamental signaling hierarchy controlling reproductive endocrine function.

Gonadorelin (GnRH)

Gonadorelin is synthetic gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH), a decapeptide (pyro-Glu-His-Trp-Ser-Tyr-Gly-Leu-Arg-Pro-Gly-NH2) secreted by hypothalamic neurons in a pulsatile manner. It acts at the GnRH receptor (GnRHR) on anterior pituitary gonadotrophs to stimulate LH and FSH synthesis and release.

Pulsatile GnRH stimulates gonadotropin release, while continuous GnRH exposure causes receptor desensitization and paradoxical suppression of LH/FSH. This biphasic pharmacology is a key area of GnRH receptor research. Gonadorelin is available in 2mg and 10mg formulations for research applications.

Triptorelin

Triptorelin is a synthetic GnRH agonist analog (pyro-Glu-His-Trp-Ser-Tyr-D-Trp-Leu-Arg-Pro-Gly-NH2) with a D-Trp substitution at position 6 that confers 100-fold greater potency than native GnRH and resistance to enzymatic degradation. Research applications focus on GnRH receptor desensitization kinetics, sustained versus pulsatile receptor activation models, and gonadotroph signaling cascade characterization.

Oxytocin

Oxytocin is a cyclic nonapeptide (Cys-Tyr-Ile-Gln-Asn-Cys-Pro-Leu-Gly-NH2) produced in the hypothalamic paraventricular and supraoptic nuclei. It acts at the oxytocin receptor (OXTR), a Gq-coupled GPCR expressed in uterine smooth muscle, mammary myoepithelial cells, and CNS regions including the amygdala and nucleus accumbens. Research applications span social bonding neuroscience, autonomic nervous system modulation, and reproductive physiology.
