Melanotan I, Melanotan II, and PT-141: Melanocortin Peptide Research Guide

Published April 5, 2026 · 6 min read

The melanocortin peptide family represents a structurally related group of synthetic alpha-MSH analogs with varying receptor selectivity profiles. This guide covers the three primary melanocortin research peptides: Melanotan I (Afamelanotide), Melanotan II, and PT-141 (Bremelanotide).

Alpha-MSH and the Melanocortin System

Endogenous alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH) is a 13 amino acid peptide derived from pro-opiomelanocortin (POMC) cleavage. It acts at five melanocortin receptor subtypes (MC1R through MC5R), each mediating distinct physiological functions. MC1R is expressed predominantly in melanocytes and mediates pigmentation. MC3R and MC4R are expressed in the hypothalamus and are involved in energy homeostasis and autonomic signaling. MC5R is found in sebaceous glands and exocrine tissues.

Melanotan I (Afamelanotide)

Melanotan I is a 13 amino acid linear analog of α-MSH with a single substitution (Nle4) that confers enhanced enzymatic stability. It maintains broad melanocortin receptor activity with moderate selectivity for MC1R. Research applications focus on MC1R-mediated signaling cascades, eumelanin synthesis pathway activation, and photoprotection mechanisms in skin cell culture models.

Melanotan II

Melanotan II is a cyclic heptapeptide (Ac-Nle-c[Asp-His-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys]-NH2) designed for enhanced receptor binding through conformational constraint. The cyclization dramatically increases binding affinity across MC1R, MC3R, MC4R, and MC5R subtypes. This non-selective profile makes Melanotan II valuable for comparative receptor binding studies and structure-activity relationship (SAR) research.

PT-141 (Bremelanotide)

PT-141 is a cyclic heptapeptide metabolite of Melanotan II with the sequence Ac-Nle-c[Asp-His-D-Phe-Arg-Trp-Lys]-OH. The critical structural difference is the C-terminal free acid versus amide, which shifts the receptor selectivity profile toward MC4R and MC3R. PT-141 research focuses on hypothalamic MC4R signaling, autonomic nervous system modulation, and central versus peripheral melanocortin pathway differentiation.

Structure-Activity Relationships

The melanocortin peptide series provides an excellent model system for studying how subtle structural modifications alter receptor selectivity. The progression from linear (MT-I) to cyclic (MT-II) to C-terminal modified (PT-141) demonstrates how backbone constraint and terminal group chemistry influence binding pocket interactions at GPCR targets.
