Melanotan I, Melanotan II, and PT-141: Melanocortin Receptor Research

Published by Reptides.co Research Team · For in-vitro research reference only

Melanotan I, Melanotan II, and PT-141 (bremelanotide) are synthetic analogs of alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH) that target melanocortin receptors (MCRs). These five G-protein-coupled receptors (MC1R through MC5R) mediate diverse signaling pathways across multiple tissue types.

Melanocortin Receptor System

The five melanocortin receptors are expressed across different tissue types and mediate distinct signaling cascades:

MC1R — expressed in melanocytes. Primary receptor in pigmentation signaling studies. Activation increases cAMP and stimulates eumelanin synthesis pathways.

MC2R — ACTH receptor. Expressed primarily in adrenal cortex. Not significantly activated by melanotan analogs.

MC3R and MC4R — expressed in hypothalamic neurons. Central to energy homeostasis and feeding behavior studies. PT-141 shows preferential affinity for these receptors.

MC5R — expressed in exocrine glands. Studied in sebaceous gland secretion models.

Research Applications

The availability of linear (MT-I), cyclic non-selective (MT-II), and cyclic receptor-preferring (PT-141) analogs enables systematic receptor pharmacology studies. Researchers can compare non-selective vs selective MCR activation, examine the impact of linear vs cyclic structure on receptor binding kinetics, and investigate the contribution of specific MCR subtypes to downstream signaling cascades using receptor-specific antagonists.

Availability

Melanotan I (10mg), Melanotan II (10mg), and PT-141 (10mg) are available as lyophilized research-grade compounds. All three are α-MSH analogs sharing the core Nle⁴-D-Phe⁷ modification that distinguishes synthetic melanocortin agonists from the native hormone sequence.
