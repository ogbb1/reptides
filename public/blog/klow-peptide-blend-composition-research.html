KLOW Peptide Blend: Composition, Components, and Research Profile

Published by Reptides.co Research Team · For in-vitro research reference only

KLOW is a four-component research peptide blend containing KPV, BPC-157, GHK-Cu, and TB-500 in a single lyophilized formulation. It is designed for researchers studying multi-peptide interactions and synergistic pathway modulation in cell culture systems.

Component Research Profiles

KPV (Lys-Pro-Val)

KPV is the C-terminal tripeptide fragment of alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH), corresponding to positions 11-13. In cell culture models, KPV has been studied for its interaction with NF-κB transcription factor signaling, a pathway central to inflammatory cascade regulation.

BPC-157

A 15 amino acid pentadecapeptide (molecular weight ~1,419 Da) with the notable characteristic of stability in acidic environments. Over 100 published studies have examined BPC-157 in various in-vitro and preclinical models investigating nitric oxide synthase pathways and growth factor receptor interactions.

GHK-Cu

A copper-chelating tripeptide with documented effects on over 4,000 genes in fibroblast gene expression profiling studies. GHK-Cu is the largest component by mass in the KLOW formulation (50mg), reflecting its established research profile in ECM signaling studies.

TB-500

A 43 amino acid synthetic fragment of thymosin beta-4 containing the actin-binding domain (residues 17-23). Studied for its role in actin polymerization regulation and cell motility in various tissue culture systems.

KLOW vs GLOW

GLOW is a three-component blend containing BPC-157 (10mg), GHK-Cu (50mg), and TB-500 (10mg) — essentially KLOW without the KPV component. Researchers investigating the contribution of α-MSH-derived signaling to multi-peptide protocols may compare KLOW and GLOW results to isolate the KPV variable.
